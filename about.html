What is NASA TLX?

The NASA Task Load Index (TLX) is a widely-used, multidimensional rating procedure that provides an overall workload score based on a weighted average of ratings on six subscales:

  • Mental Demand: How much mental and perceptual activity was required?
  • Physical Demand: How much physical activity was required?
  • Temporal Demand: How much time pressure did you feel?
  • Performance: How successful were you in accomplishing the task?
  • Effort: How hard did you have to work to accomplish your level of performance?
  • Frustration: How insecure, discouraged, irritated, stressed, and annoyed were you?

History and Development

The NASA TLX was developed by Sandra G. Hart and Lowell E. Staveland at NASA Ames Research Center in the 1980s. It was created to provide a reliable and valid measure of workload that could be applied across various domains and tasks.

How to Use NASA TLX - Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Task Selection: Choose the task or set of tasks you want to evaluate.
  2. Rating Scale: Ask participants to rate the task on the six subscales.
  3. Weighting Procedure: Determine the importance of each subscale for the specific task.
  4. Data Analysis: Calculate the overall workload score using the weighted ratings.

FAQs

  • What is the NASA TLX used for? NASA TLX is used to measure the perceived workload of individuals performing a wide range of tasks. It helps identify areas where performance may be compromised due to high workload.
  • Who can use NASA TLX? Researchers, professionals, and organizations across various fields including psychology, human factors, ergonomics, and more.
  • Is there a digital version of NASA TLX? Yes, digital tools and apps are available for administering NASA TLX and analyzing the data.
